Two resource pictures for the same deliverable set. The solo build's direct cost was $160 — eight weeks of a Claude subscription, everything else on free tiers. That figure is striking but misleading in isolation: the dominant cost of any L&D project is labor, and here the practitioner's roughly 150–160 focused hours were uncosted. The organizational estimate restores the full-cost picture: $191,000–$271,000 for a five-person team delivering the same scope to 200 participants.

Salary estimates use market rates for mid-to-senior L&D and engineering roles in major metros (Singapore, Hong Kong, or comparable); an adopting organization would substitute its own loaded rates.

## Where the money goes

Labor accounts for 82–85% of the organizational total, consistent with L&D benchmarks. The front-end developer is the single largest line item, because the custom platform is the most labor-intensive deliverable. An organization using an off-the-shelf LMS or authoring tool would eliminate that line — and lose the interactive components (data dashboards, tokenizer playground, AI sandbox) that differentiate the program. That tradeoff is the subject of the panel below.
