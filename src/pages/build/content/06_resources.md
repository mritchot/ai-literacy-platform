The solo build's direct cost was $260: about $220 of Claude subscriptions (Pro for the first two weeks, then Max 5x) plus a Google AI Pro plan for NotebookLM, with everything else on free tiers. That figure is striking but misleading in isolation: the dominant cost of any L&D project is labor, and here the practitioner's roughly 150–160 focused hours were uncosted, an approximate opportunity cost of about $7,500. The organizational estimate is an approximate sample that restores the full-cost picture: roughly $190,000–$258,000 for a five-person team delivering the same scope to 200 participants, showing the shape of the cost rather than a number to commit to.

Salary estimates are fully loaded (base plus benefits, taxes, and overhead, roughly 1.4×) and use market rates for mid-to-senior L&D and engineering roles in major metros (Singapore, Hong Kong, or comparable); an adopting organization would substitute its own.

## Where the money goes

Labor accounts for about 80% of the organizational total, consistent with L&D benchmarks. The full-stack developer is the single largest line item, because the custom platform is the most labor-intensive deliverable. An organization using an off-the-shelf LMS or authoring tool would eliminate that line, and lose the interactive components (data dashboards, tokenizer playground, AI sandbox) that differentiate the program.
